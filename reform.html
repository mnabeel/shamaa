Reform

Transforming Knowledge Through Observation, Experimentation, and Reason

The Scientific Revolution marked a major reform in how knowledge was produced and understood, transforming intellectual life by replacing reliance on tradition, religious authority, and classical texts with observation, experimentation, and reason. Scholars began using the scientific method—systematic observation, hypothesis testing, and experimentation—to evaluate ideas, which encouraged skepticism and evidence-based conclusions rather than unquestioned acceptance. This reform led to significant advancements in astronomy through the heliocentric model proposed by Copernicus and supported by Galileo and Kepler, in physics through Isaac Newton's laws of motion and universal gravitation, in biology through improved understanding of human anatomy based on direct study, and in mathematics through the development of calculus. Beyond specific discoveries, the Scientific Revolution reformed society's worldview by promoting rationalism and secular thinking, laying the foundation for technological innovation and modern science, and reshaping education, philosophy, and the way humans understood their place in the natural world.

Copernicus, Galileo, and the Church: Science in a Religious World

The tension between scientific discovery and religious authority defined much of the Scientific Revolution era, as scholars navigated the challenging landscape of faith and reason.

Key Aspects of the Reform

  • Scientific Method: Systematic observation, hypothesis testing, and experimentation
  • Empiricism Over Authority: Evidence-based conclusions replacing unquestioned acceptance
  • Astronomical Advancements: Heliocentric model by Copernicus, supported by Galileo and Kepler
  • Physics Revolution: Newton's laws of motion and universal gravitation
  • Biological Understanding: Improved human anatomy through direct study
  • Mathematical Development: Creation of calculus
  • Worldview Transformation: Promotion of rationalism and secular thinking
  • Foundation for Progress: Basis for technological innovation and modern science
